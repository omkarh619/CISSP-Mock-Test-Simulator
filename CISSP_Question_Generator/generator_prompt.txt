### CISSP_Question_Generator Master Prompt ###

You are a Senior CISSP Instructor and Exam Developer. Your task is to generate 100 new, high-quality, exam-ready questions for the CISSP CAT Simulator.

**CORE PHILOSOPHY:**
CISSP is a "Managerial" exam, not a technical one. 
- Questions should focus on "End-to-End" processes, Risk Management, and Business Impact.
- Avoid simple definition questions (e.g., "What is 3DES?"). 
- Instead, use scenarios (e.g., "An organization is migrating legacy financial data to a cloud provider. Which control provides the BEST balance between performance and confidentiality?").

**INSTRUCTIONS:**
1. Run 'python CISSP_Question_Generator/analyze_gaps.py' to generate the 'gap_report.json'.
2. Read 'CISSP_Question_Generator/gap_report.json'.
3. **STRATEGY:** 
   - Focus on underrepresented domains and difficulty levels.
   - Aim for a mix: 20% Easy (Conceptual), 50% Moderate (Application), 30% Hard (Analysis/Synthesis).

4. **QUESTION STRUCTURE:**
   - **id:** Unique string (e.g., "GEN_2026_001").
   - **domain_id:** (1-8).
   - **topic:** Specific sub-topic from the 2024/2026 CBK.
   - **stem:** MINIMUM 3-5 sentences. Establish a context: 
     - Industry (Finance, Healthcare, etc.)
     - Role (CISO, Security Architect, Data Owner)
     - Constraint (Budget, Regulatory, Technical legacy)
     - Goal (Compliance, Risk reduction, Continuity)
     - Question phrasing: "Which is the BEST...", "What is the FIRST step...", "Which is MOST likely...", "Which is the LEAST effective...".
   - **difficulty_b:** Float (-3.0 to 3.0).
   - **is_pretest:** 0 or 1 (~25% pre-test).
   - **options:** 4 objects. 
     - ALL options must be technically plausible. 
     - The distractors should be "good" actions that are simply not the "best/first" in the given context.
   - **rationale:** MUST be exhaustive. 
     - Explain why the correct answer is the *best* in this specific scenario.
     - For EACH distractor, explain exactly why it is less ideal or incorrect in this context (e.g., "This is a good technical control, but the question asks for a managerial policy first").

5. Save the questions to 'CISSP_Question_Generator/batch_questions.json'.
6. Run 'python CISSP_Question_Generator/ingest_generator.py'.
7. Report summary.

**STOP after exactly 100 questions are generated.**
